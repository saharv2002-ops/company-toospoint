%% ── Executive Summary ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Executive Summary}

I am pleased to submit this proposal to partner with \textbf{One World Global}
(``OWG'') on a fractional basis, focused on strengthening the interpreter
pipeline, improving interpreter engagement and retention, scaling operational
delivery, and supporting client acquisition and growth.

The language services market rewards two capabilities above all others:
\textbf{coverage you can count on} and \textbf{interpreters who stay}. This
engagement is built to move both metrics in measurable ways, while creating
the operational scaffolding OWG needs to absorb new client volume without
service degradation.

\begin{clausebox}
\sffamily\small
\textbf{The thesis:} OWG's next phase of growth is gated less by demand and
more by the strength, responsiveness, and scalability of its interpreter
operations. This engagement is structured to remove that gate.
\end{clausebox}

%% ── Engagement Overview ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Engagement Overview}

I will provide \textbf{fractional operational and growth support} for One
World Global, anchored across four interlocking focus areas:

\vspace{4pt}
\pillar{1. Interpreter Recruitment}{Pipeline strategy, sourcing execution, and language-demand alignment}
\pillar{2. Interpreter Engagement \& Care}{Retention, communication, responsiveness, and onboarding experience}
\pillar{3. Operational Support \& Scaling}{Workflows, coverage optimization, and vendor structure}
\pillar{4. Client Acquisition \& Growth}{Positioning, onboarding readiness, and scaling go-to-market capacity}
\vspace{4pt}

This engagement is designed to operate \textbf{inside} OWG's day-to-day
rather than alongside it --- producing tangible operational outputs, not just
recommendations.

%% ── Scope of Support ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Scope of Support}

\subsection{1. Interpreter Recruitment Strategy \& Execution}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=3pt, topsep=2pt]
  \item Recruitment planning aligned to language demand patterns and
        operational coverage gaps
  \item Recruitment workflow optimization (intake $\rightarrow$ vetting
        $\rightarrow$ onboarding $\rightarrow$ activation)
  \item Hands-on interpreter sourcing support across priority languages
  \item Outreach strategy design --- channels, messaging, and sequencing
  \item Identification and targeted sourcing for priority and
        low-diffusion languages
  \item Recruitment process scalability recommendations as request volume
        grows
\end{itemize}

\subsection{2. Interpreter Engagement \& Interpreter Care}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=3pt, topsep=2pt]
  \item Interpreter engagement initiatives that drive retention and
        affinity
  \item Retention strategy development informed by interpreter feedback
        and attrition signals
  \item Interpreter communication workflows (announcements, updates,
        coverage requests)
  \item Coverage and responsiveness improvement strategies
  \item Best practices around interpreter onboarding experience
  \item Support with interpreter performance and engagement monitoring
\end{itemize}

\subsection{3. Operational Support \& Scaling}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=3pt, topsep=2pt]
  \item Operational workflow recommendations across the interpreter
        lifecycle
  \item Onboarding process improvement (time-to-first-call, completion
        rates, compliance)
  \item Queue and coverage optimization guidance
  \item Vendor and interpreter operational structure recommendations
  \item Escalation and communication process support
\end{itemize}

\subsection{4. Client Acquisition \& Growth Support}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=3pt, topsep=2pt]
  \item Client-facing operational positioning and messaging
  \item Onboarding readiness for new clients (capacity, language coverage,
        SLA posture)
  \item Strategic support for scaling operational capacity to match
        commercial growth
  \item Support with presentations, messaging, and growth initiatives as
        needed
\end{itemize}

%% ── Approach ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Approach \& Working Model}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=4pt, topsep=4pt]
  \item \textbf{Embedded, not external.} I operate as a fractional
        extension of OWG's team --- participating in workflows, not
        observing them.
  \item \textbf{Outcome-led.} Each focus area is tracked against agreed
        operational signals (e.g., active interpreters, coverage rates,
        response times, onboarding velocity).
  \item \textbf{Cadence-driven.} Weekly working sessions, monthly
        priority reviews, and asynchronous execution between touchpoints.
  \item \textbf{Adaptive scope.} The engagement adjusts to operational
        priorities as they evolve through the quarter.
\end{itemize}

%% ── What Success Looks Like ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{What Success Looks Like}

While precise targets will be calibrated jointly during the first two weeks
of the engagement, success is anticipated to be measured against signals
such as:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=3pt, topsep=2pt]
  \item Growth in active, deployable interpreters --- with particular
        emphasis on priority and low-diffusion languages
  \item Reduction in coverage gaps and improved interpreter response times
